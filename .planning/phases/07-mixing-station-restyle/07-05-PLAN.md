  <how-to-verify>
    **MIX-01 — Squircle cards with gradient waveform + elevated drag shadow**
    1. Look at any clip card. Confirm:
       - Corner radius is clearly rounder than v1.0 (24pt continuous squircle, not 12pt).
       - No visible 1pt Indigo stroke border around the card at rest.
       - Drop shadow is softer (black@0.10) — no harsh dark halo.
    2. Touch-and-hold a clip card (don't reorder yet — just press). Confirm:
       - The card scales up subtly (about 3%).
       - The shadow turns Deep Indigo and grows (glow radius jumps from 16→24).
       - A medium haptic fires on finger-down.
    3. Release. Confirm:
       - Card returns to rest size + black shadow with spring animation.
       - A light haptic fires on release.
    4. Look at the waveform thumbnail inside the card. Confirm:
       - Bars show a clear Deep-Indigo-to-Purple diagonal gradient (top-leading indigo → bottom-trailing purple).
       - On light mode: bars sit over an off-white well.
       - On dark mode: bars sit over a near-black well.

    **MIX-02 — Vertical Timeline Hybrid central line**
    5. Confirm with 3+ clips loaded: a thin 2pt Deep-Indigo line is visible running vertically down the left side of the clip stack, at roughly the horizontal center of the waveform thumbnails.
    6. Confirm the line appears continuous across clip cards (passing behind them).
    7. Confirm the line appears to "thread through" the `+` operator circles between clips — the circle background is opaque, not translucent.
    8. Confirm the line is visible in the whitespace of the gap row above/below each pill.
    9. Delete clips one by one until only 1 remains. Confirm the central line disappears entirely.

    **MIX-03 — Mesh gradient on iOS 18 / LinearGradient on iOS 17**
    10. On the iPhone 17 simulator (iOS 18+), screenshot a clip card. Gradient should show subtle 2D curvature (mesh interpolation).
    11. If you have an iOS 17 simulator available (e.g. iPhone 15 / iOS 17.x), re-run `xcodebuild -destination 'platform=iOS Simulator,OS=17.5,name=iPhone 15'` and screenshot the same card. Gradient should be a clean diagonal LinearGradient, visually acceptable side-by-side.
    12. If no iOS 17 simulator is installed, explicitly note this in the summary and mark MIX-03 as "approved via code-path inspection only" (the `#available(iOS 18.0, *)` guard is grep-verifiable).

    **MIX-04 — Gap row pill buttons**
    13. Look at a gap row between two clips. Confirm 4 pill buttons: `0.5s`, `1.0s`, `2.0s`, `Crossfade`.
    14. Confirm exactly one pill is filled Deep Indigo (selected) and the other three are outlined with a 0.5-opacity Indigo stroke.
    15. Tap a different pill. Confirm:
        - Selection moves.
        - Light haptic fires.
        - The gap duration change persists — close and reopen the Mixing Station (or just observe that the selected pill stays on the tapped option).
    16. Confirm the row background is transparent — the central spine is still visible above and below the pill row.

    **MIX-05 — Drag micro-animation**
    17. Touch-and-hold a clip card and drag it to a new position (reorder). Confirm:
        - Card scales up to 1.03 during the drag.
        - Shadow elevates to Deep Indigo glow.
        - System reorder indicator shows correctly.
    18. **CRITICAL: Drag the 2nd clip to position 0** (top of the stack). Confirm the app does NOT crash. Repeat with the 3rd clip → position 1. This exercises the reorder-crash-fix invariant.
    19. Drop the clip. Confirm card returns to rest state smoothly.

    **Accessibility smoke tests**
    20. Settings → Accessibility → Motion → Reduce Motion → ON. Return to the app. Confirm:
        - Drag scale change is instant (no spring).
        - PillButtonStyle press scale does not animate.
        - Nothing crashes.
    21. Settings → Accessibility → Display & Text Size → Reduce Transparency → ON. Return to the app. Confirm:
        - Central spine line is visibly darker (0.55 vs 0.35 opacity).
        - Glassmorphism trust strip falls back to a solid card background.
    22. Switch system appearance Dark → Light → Dark. Confirm gradient stops and spine both render correctly in both modes.
